# Cover letter — what to write, what to never write

## The easiest fix
Cover letters addressed "Dear Hiring Manager" are ~30% more likely to be rejected.
The fix takes 90 seconds:
- If a human contact is listed for the job, use their name.
- For smaller companies, find the recruiter or hiring manager on LinkedIn.
- Check the company's careers page for a name.
- Triple-check the company name. Don't copy-paste a mistake from your last application.

## The structure — four paragraphs, that's it
1. **Introduce yourself** — one sentence. Who you are, what role, any connection
   (referral, friend of a friend).
2. **Expand** — three sentences max. Build on the intro. Don't repeat your resume.
3. **Why you, specifically** — three sentences max. Reference something specific to
   this company: a recent product, a public statement, news.
4. **Promise to follow up** — buys you the right to follow up later without feeling
   annoying.

## The non-negotiable: customize every time
A customized cover letter signals you put in real work. A generic one signals you
didn't.

The workflow that works:
- Write one base letter you're proud of.
- Use AI to customize paragraph 3 for each job.
- Read it once out loud before sending. Rewrite it in your own voice to catch typos
  and AI hallucinations.
