# Interview + practice

## The interview secret
Being "qualified" isn't enough — everyone in the room is qualified. You win on
connection and clear, structured answers.

### Small talk is the best talk
Stay (politely) off-topic for as long as you can. The interviewer doesn't get to
make a friend with the next ten candidates — they get to make one with you. Use
the topics you seeded in your cover letter or intro to start it.

### The first interview might not be with a human
More companies use AI interviewers for round one. Treat it like a real interview:
- Speak clearly, at a normal pace — most AIs transcribe first, evaluate second.
- Use structure — AI scoring rewards clear, organized answers.
- Keep notes on Post-Its clustered near the camera, not in a doc. Eye movement is
  obvious to the humans who review.

### Five things that slash your odds (instant rejections)
- Trash-talking your last employer — even if they deserve it. *Especially* then.
- Lying about a skill or experience — it comes out in week one.
- Showing up late — there's no recovering. Be there 15 minutes early.
- Asking about salary first — wait until they bring it up.
- No questions at the end — always have 3 ready.

## Practice or perish (~13.5 hours that separate winners from losers)

### 12 questions you'll almost certainly be asked
1. Tell me about yourself.
2. Why this role?
3. Why this company?
4. What's your greatest strength?
5. What's your greatest weakness?
6. Tell me about a time you led a team.
7. Tell me about a time you failed.
8. Tell me about a difficult coworker.
9. Why are you leaving your current job?
10. Where do you see yourself in 5 years?
11. What are your salary expectations?
12. Do you have any questions for us?

Brainstorm the questions you think you'll get — aim for ~30.

### Craft 2 answers per question
Each interviewer evaluates a different mix of attributes. If a later interviewer
asks something you've been asked before, give your *second* answer. Two answers
ready means even when one doesn't land, the next one might.

### One answer structure to rule them all
1. **Restate the question** — buys you 3 seconds to think.
2. **Summarize what you're about to say** — tells the listener where you're going.
3. **Tell a story illustrating the trait** — specific, with numbers when possible.
4. **Summarize the story/insight again** — lands the point.

> "Tell them what you'll say. Say it. Tell them what you said."

Then: write 2 answers each, and practice 3x out loud.

## How to coach this
Run a mock interview. Ask one question at a time, have the user answer, then score
it against the 4-part structure and push for a number or specific story. Help them
build the second answer for each.
