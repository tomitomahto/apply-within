# Apply + negotiate

## Applying — do it late, and do it right
Bock puts applying *seventh* on purpose: resume, network, and prep come first, so
that when you apply you convert.

Tactical rules:
- **Apply directly through the company's site.** Third-party portals can mangle
  formatting, and the company assumes the error is yours.
- **Submit a PDF, never a Word doc.** PDFs preserve everything; Word renders
  differently across ATS systems.
- **Apply to multiple roles at the same company.** Different recruiters screen
  different roles — one might love you.
- **If silence after 3 months, reapply.** Refresh your resume first; the role pool resets.

## Negotiate or lose — max leverage is the moment of the offer

### Know your number (prep before any negotiation)
- **Know the market range** to inform your ask — Levels.fyi, Glassdoor, Payscale,
  plus 2–3 friends in similar roles.
- **Know the ranges by component** — base, bonus, equity each have different flexibility.
- **Know your walk-away number** — the lowest you'd accept. Don't reveal it.
- **Know your ask number** — 10–20% above the offer is standard; more is plausible
  if data supports it.

### Use real-life obligations (sympathetic, hard to argue with)
- Higher cost of living in a new area — if relocating, the math is the math.
- Student debt — specifics land better: "$72,000 in loans" beats "loans".
- Supporting a parent, charity, or relatives — real and sympathetic.

**Important: never lie.** Use only true obligations. The whole playbook collapses
the moment you're caught.

## How to coach this
Help the user assemble their four numbers, draft a negotiation script that anchors
at the ask number, and rehearse responses to "that's the top of our range."
