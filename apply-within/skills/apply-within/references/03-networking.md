# Networking without a network

Referrals get hired 5–10x more often than cold applicants. The goal of networking
is a referral and inside knowledge — not "coffee".

## No network? Start where people gather
- **Industry meetups** — search Eventbrite or Meetup for your city + field.
- **LinkedIn comments** — comment thoughtfully on posts from people in your target role.
- **Slack or Discord communities** — most industries have one. Join three, be useful in two.
- **Alumni groups** — school alumni clubs are designed for exactly this.

## 5 questions that get people hired
Ask these in conversations — they open doors without asking for a job outright:
1. **How did you get your job?** — everyone loves telling their origin story.
2. **What advice would you give someone trying to break into your field/company?**
   — their answer is usually the playbook for that company.
3. **What parts of your company are hiring right now?** — they may know about
   openings before they're posted.
4. **Could you refer me to the recruiter?** — many companies pay referral bonuses,
   so this helps them too.
5. **Do you know someone else who could give me advice?** — one conversation becomes
   three, three becomes ten, and one of those ten is hiring.
