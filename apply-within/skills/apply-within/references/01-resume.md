# Resume — the X/Y/Z rewrite

## Why most resumes fail
They list duties, not results. A recruiter spends ~6 seconds per resume. Vague
duties ("Managed budget", "Member of the customer experience team", "Helped lead
key initiatives") tell them nothing. Numbers do.

## The only formula that works
> **Accomplished [X] as measured by [Y] by doing [Z]**

- **X** — what you achieved
- **Y** — how it was measured (numbers, %, $, time)
- **Z** — how you got it done

### Worked examples
Student:
- Original: "Managed sorority budget."
- Better: "Managed $31,000 Spring budget and invested idle funds."
- Awesome: "Managed $31,000 Spring budget and invested $10,000 of idle funds in
  high-yield capital notes returning 5% over the year."

Professional:
- Original: "Responsible for negotiating service contract with Salesforce."
- Better: "Negotiated 30% ($500k) reduction in costs with Salesforce for
  post-delivery support."
- Awesome: "Negotiated 30% ($500k) reduction with Salesforce for post-delivery
  support by designing and using results from an online auction of multiple vendors."

When coaching: take each bullet to the "Awesome" tier, or name the missing
element (X, Y, or Z) and ask the user for it.

## 5 formatting rules most people break
1. Save as a PDF — other formats get mangled by recruiting software.
2. 11pt minimum font — smaller and recruiters can't read it.
3. ½" margins — tighter margins = more room for content.
4. No tables or columns — recruiting software breaks them.
5. Contact info on every page — if a page gets separated, you're still findable.

## Beat the ATS (97% of Fortune 500 screen with AI first)
1. Mirror the job description's exact phrases in real bullets (e.g. if the post
   says "stakeholder management", use that phrase).
2. Spell out acronyms once: "Search Engine Optimization (SEO)" — both forms match.
3. Run it through a free ATS scanner; aim for 75%+ match. Tools: Jobscan,
   Resume Worded, Enhancv.
